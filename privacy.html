Privacy Policy

Slings and Arrows · Effective June 28, 2026

Slings and Arrows has no accounts and no sign-in, and we run no servers of our own. The game is free and supported by ads, so the one place information is involved is our advertising provider, as described below.

Information we collect ourselves

None. We do not operate any servers, and the developer does not receive or store any information that identifies you.

Data stored on your device

Your high scores and game settings (such as aim sensitivity and handedness) are saved locally on your device using the system’s standard app storage, so the game remembers your preferences between sessions. This information never leaves your device and is removed if you delete the app.

Advertising

The game shows occasional full-screen ads between rounds through Unity Ads, a service provided by Unity Technologies. To deliver and measure those ads, Unity may collect technical information such as your device’s advertising identifier, IP address, device type and operating system, and how you interact with ads.

On first launch, iOS will ask whether you allow the app to track you. If you allow it, ads may be personalized using your device’s advertising identifier. If you decline, you’ll still see ads, but they won’t be personalized. You can change this choice at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.

How Unity handles this data is described in the Unity Privacy Policy.

Children’s privacy

Slings and Arrows is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we’ll update this page and revise the effective date above.
